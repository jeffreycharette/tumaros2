PortfolioCenter 5.4 Coming Soon—Sign Up for the Release

Watch for the PortfolioCenter 5.4 release in November. With the introduction of asset-class performance pages, this release will further expand the PortfolioCenter client presentation capabilities. Additionally, the release will feature several important cost basis enhancements to help you prepare for the next phase of the regulations, including:

  • A lot-level reconciliation tool that simplifies the task of identifying and correcting out-of-balance positions
  • Cost Basis Reset Wizard enhancements that streamline the process of updating cost basis data in PortfolioCenter to match your custodian
  • Expanded capabilities to support the second phase of the cost basis regulations, which take effect on January 1, 2012, and cover mutual funds, dividend reinvestment plans, and exchange traded funds

To learn more about the cost basis features in PortfolioCenter 5.4 and for an overview of the cost basis changes coming in 2012, register for our December 7 webcast. You can also sign up to be notified when PortfolioCenter 5.4 is available for download.

For additional cost basis resources, visit the cost basis page on schwabpt.com/costbasis.

Introducing Schwab OpenView Integrated Office™

Last year, Schwab Advisor Services™ announced Schwab Intelligent Integration™, a multiyear initiative to help advisors improve integration between the disparate systems and workflows in their office. Schwab Intelligent Technologies™ is the new company that will work with best-in-class technology providers to create intelligent integration solutions for advisors.

As a participating provider, Schwab Performance Technologies is excited to introduce Schwab OpenView Integrated Office, a turnkey platform that brings together Salesforce® client relationship management (CRM) customized for advisors who custody with Schwab and our own PortfolioServices outsourced portfolio accounting and performance reporting solution.

Advisors using this prepackaged, integrated platform will be able to see real-time Schwab custody data—such as balances, transactions, holdings, and alerts—directly within the CRM. And because the outsourced portfolio management is based on PortfolioCenter technology and supported by our industry-leading service teams, it’s a great option for advisors who currently use PortfolioCenter and are looking for a simpler, hosted solution for their firm.

Schwab OpenView Integrated Office is currently in pilot—watch for general availability in the coming months. For more information, visit the Schwab Intelligent Technologies website or contact SPT sales.

Understanding Partial Wash Sales

If you reconcile cost basis regularly, you will likely notice a small cost basis variance for some positions and a wash sale indicator on the cost basis reconciliation report. A wash sale occurs when you realize a loss on the sale of a position, but buy that position again within 30 days of the sale. Smaller variances with the wash sale indicator usually occur when your client receives reinvested income either 30 days before the sale or receives a trailing reinvested dividend 30 days after the sale.
