The new space will include:

Twice the exhibition space with the flexibility to configure three to five individual galleries.

A project gallery will be dedicated to technology-based art and small-scale installations

The ICA Print Center, which will accommodate up to six printmakers at one time, represents a one-of-a-kind program in the South Bay, providing on-going group and individual workshops to printmakers throughout the region in addition to four individual artist residencies per year.

Improved infrastructure: Heat, air-conditioning and six bathrooms!

Commenting on the acquisition of the building at 560 South First Street, Executive Director Cathy Kimball said, "This new permanent location affords the ICA the capability of moving confidently into the future on firm ground. It is tremendously exciting for all of us to realize this next step in the ICA's history."

During the construction, the galleries are closed and the administrative offices have been temporarily relocated. The public may still contact the ICA at 408.283.8155.

During the dark period, the ICA has designed a website to track the progress of the construction and the capital campaign at www.comfortableprovocation.org.
