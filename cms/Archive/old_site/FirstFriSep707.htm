Come see what's happening for First Friday, September 7th
In San Jose's SoFA District

South FIRST FRIDAYS September 7th

San Jose Institute of Contemporary Art -560 South First Street
NextNew2007 - The ICA's annual exhibition features the next new talent on the horizon. This year's artists have been chosen by faculty members from MFA programs throughout California. In the Focus Gallery is the ICA's NextNew pick, aritst Mitra Fabian. Mitra Fabian transforms media like scotch tape, glue and paper into sprawling floor works. Also on view, NIGHT MOVES: A series featuring window installations visible only after dark at the ICA: Jennifer Brandon: Fallen and Keturah Cummings: Liten Gygr.

6-9pm - AD's Street Mrkt featuring Day One Symphony hardworking San Jose locals who specialize in ambient rock; Worker Bee fresh off of a Pacific Northwest tour in support of their debut record "Divorce Your Legs" in stores now; Doctor Nurse San Jose's best kept secret, a no-frills rock band with a loyal following. Also showcasing art and creative indie products by local artist vendors.

8pm til late - SoFA Gallery Walk
The SoFA District is So. First Street between San Carlos and E. Reed streets

Anno Domini - 366 South First Street
Opening Reception: A Summer Cut for Medusa's Daughters a new body of work and third Anno Domini exhibition by mythical painter Alfonso Kellenberger. Featuring MERCH an experimental-pop cello-rock band from San Francisco that has made many waves in a short amount of time with their ultra-catchy rocking songs. In October, MERCH will be releasing their first album 'Crash Boom Bash'--songs dealing with hopping freight trains, throwing water balloons at yuppies, sorcerors, and more.

MACLA - 510 South First Street
Opening Reception: MACLA is pleased to announce its second Chicana/o Biennial -- an exhibition and public forum conceived to take inventory of and invite reflection every two years on the continuously emergent energy, critical edge, and aesthetic interventions within contemporary Chicano art.

San Jose Museum of Quilts & Textiles -520 South First Street
On view: Weavings of War: Fabrics of Memory a traveling exhibition of textile works by mostly women from around the world depicting their reactions to war, Woven Witness: Afghan War Rugs an exhibition that explores the influence of war on traditional Afghan rug design, and Patriot Art, an exhibit that reflects on the uses of patriotic imagery and allusion in both contemporary fiber art and historical textiles.

First Friday evening will be an informal question and answer session about the Afghan Freedom Quilt: Silenced Voices of the Afghan Diaspora and Afghan War Rugs with: Melanie Gadener, founding director of the Foundation for Self-Reliance and Hanifa Tokhi, board member at the Foundation for Self-Reliance and long-time activist on behalf of Afghan refugees.

Works San Jose - 451 South First Street
Opening Reception: eXact-O! - The art of the precision cut with Jesse Houlding, Marina Shterenberg, Charlene Tan, and Annie Vought.

--------------- OTHER FIRST FRIDAY ART EVENTS ---------------

KALEID gallery - 88 South Fourth Street (at San Fernando)
7pm-9pm - Featured Artists' Reception My Neighbors a photographic tribute to the smaller side of Silicon Valley business by Joe Claus; also featured About Face paintings of solitary figures by Mary Medrano that represent interaction, communication and love after the loss of her mother last year. Music by DJ Oh & Lynn Ness direct from the New Medina collective, spinning the sweetest hard-bop and soul-jazz delicacies from the '60s.

Hally K Ono Cafe - 312 South Third Street (at San Carlos)
7pm-9pm - Featured Artist: Sushanta, who is inspired by colors, forms and nature. Enjoy paintings that are rich, vibrant, responsive and energetic reflecting the many moods of life and her varied interests.

Art Object Gallery- 592 North Fifth Street (Japantown)
5pm-9pm - This 1st ever Artist's Studio Inventory Sale (thru Aug 4) will feature special pricing allowing everyone an excellent opportunity to show support for our local artists and for the creative climate in Japantown. Among the exhibiting artists: Kelly Detweiller, Jaap Bongers, Terry Kreiter, Diane Kreiter, Debi Lorenc, Victoria May, Randall Shiroma, Diane Levinson, Joe Saxe, Renee Billinglea, Mario Uribe, Erin Goodwin-Guerro, Reid Winfrey, Duncan McCandless, Ken Matsumoto, Kathy Aoki, Harry and Lynn Powers, Margaret Michel, Will Johnson, Pancho Jimenez, and others.

commuknity's Expressions gallery - 1345 The Alameda
7pm-9pm - Artists reception for the new group exhibit, Fiber & Fauna… a mixed media menagerie. The show features works by Liz Siegel, Sue Atwell, Patricia La Fleur, Cathy Eisemann and Ivy Mok.
