SubZERO is part of the second biennial of 01SJ Global Art on the Edge. This one-night interactive event is happening on South First Street and includes over 50 local and international artists.

The ICA will host artists that utilize innovative technologies - including nomadic weaving, drawing robots and a barter-based gallery. Lacey Jane Roberts will knit using larger-than-life knitting needles with yarn she makes out of old recycled T-Shirts. Travis Meinolf will teach party goers how to use "laser looms," portable weaving system that can be attached to a tree, a bench or a street lamp to weave hand-made cloth. Peter Foucault will set up Robot Drawing Machine #8 Attraction/Repulsion, an interactive drawing "ring" where two self-propelled, motion sensor drawing robots respond to sounds that the audience makes to create an abstract drawing. The Infinite Exchange Gallery, a project organized by Lori Gordon and Jen de los Reyes, will present their nomadic gallery where artwork will be available for non-monetary barter-transactions. And finally, poet and artist Zach Houston will wander the street festival and write a poem about anything you want (for a sliding scale fee) with his green portable typewriter. John F. King II will play percussions using buckets, cans and found objects.

In celebration of the ICA's first year in the newly renovated space, Print Director and artist Fanny Retsek will set up a T-shirt screen-printing station where fair attendees will be able to print a T-shirt. The design will be made by artist and designer Joshua Swanbeck to commemerate the one year anniversary of the ICA in its new permanent home.
