March 10th
Ed Clapp
6:30 pm

 

Ed Clapp
False Memory, 2001
In conjunction with the current exhibition, In the Round: Selections from the Pacific Rim Sculptors Group, every Thursday evening artists in the exhibition will be on hand in the gallery to discuss his or her work. This week's featured artist is Ed Clapp. Admission is free. Please join us.

Ed Clapp likes the responsiveness of clay and the repetitious working, molding, and gesturing involved in the fabrication of his large-scale ceramic works. It is a slow process that requires patience, trust and a leap of faith that all will go well in the kiln. Clapp’s disembodied hands, extended in a gesture of prayer or offering, allude to his hopeful belief in the process. The oversized dimensions of Clapp’s works evoke a whimsicality that reflects his artistic spirit.
