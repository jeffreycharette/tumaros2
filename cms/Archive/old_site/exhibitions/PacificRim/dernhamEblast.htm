In conjunction with the current exhibition, In the Round: Selections from the Pacific Rim Sculptors Group, every Thursday evening artists in the exhibition will be on hand in the gallery to discuss his or her work. This week's featured artists are Pamela Merory Dernham and Jane Johnson. Admission is free. Please join us.
     

 
  Pamela Merory Dernham constructs sculptures that are made of steel and air. Her works are composed of numerous figures whose boundaries blur and overlap. The figures emerge and recede from the chaotic tangle of forms.

Jane Johnson explores the absurd and paradoxical in her sculptures without losing track of her concern for form and perfect craftsmanship. Nourished by the carving, shaping and rubbing of its surface, Johnson skillfully amplifies the inherent beauty in wood - the natural properties of color, pattern, and texture. She also works in steel and cast bronze and feels that the patience and perseverance required by these materials suits her temperament.
