Lift, 2004, Steel, 108 x 12 x 2 in.
Peter Hiers
Thursday, January 20th
6:30 pm
Budwood, 2005
Stainless Steel, 31 x 19 x 11 in.
In conjunction with the current exhibition, In the Round: Selections from the Pacific Rim Sculptors Group, every Thursday evening one of the artists in the exhibition will be on hand in the gallery to discuss his or her work. This week's featured artist is Peter Hiers. Admission is free. Please join us.
According to Hiers, his work focuses on the enigmatic energy that is inherent in all life forms. Fascinated by the evolution of all natural creation, he is interested in "what is inside, what's left after the outside layers are taken away." With this as his guiding premise, Hiers has reduced his sculptures to the bare essentials. Only the graceful curves and soaring forms remain. Void of anatomic detail, the vertical orientation and life-size scale of the work still evoke a sense of the human form. It is this suggestion of human presence that Hiers uses to capture the sacredness of life inherent in all forms as well as to emphasize the evolutionary process linking all creation across time.
