Mary V. King
6:30 pm

Mary V. King
FANBLOTTERROCKER, 2002

 
In conjunction with the current exhibition, In the Round: Selections from the Pacific Rim Sculptors Group, every Thursday evening artists in the exhibition will be on hand in the gallery to discuss his or her work. This week's featured artist is Mary V. King. Admission is free. Please join us.
Largely self-taught, when Mary V. King needs to figure something out, she often develops her own methods. Therefore, her technique is distinctive. Hear her describe how she builds her sculptures layer by layer with various laminating processes and then carves them using handheld tools: chisels, knives, a grinder and a saber saw. Wood is her primary medium, which she sometimes combines with parchment, monofilament, vellum and other materials. According to King, “Sculpture has beauty when its freshness astonishes and at the same time reassures by eliciting some inexplicable remembrance.” An avid reader and former philosophy student, she also incorporates personally significant words into many of her pieces.
