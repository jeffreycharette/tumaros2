Please join us for:

South FIRST FRIDAYS -  
an eclectic evening of arts and culture in downtown San Jose's SoFA District held on the First Friday of every month.

Friday, August 3rd: Please join us this Friday as the ICA, Anno Domini, MACLA, the Museum of Quilts and Textiles and Works/San Jose present South First Fridays.

The SoFA district has been a long-time destination for art enthusiasts in San Jose. On the First Friday of every month the neighborhood art venues join the SoFA restaurants and clubs to stay open late. Come on down for an evening of art, culture, music, dining and dancing. And, mark your calendars for the First Friday of EVERY month!
This Month's Participating Art Venues:

San Jose Institute of Contemporary Art- 560 South First Street

NextNew2007 - The ICA's annual exhibition features the next new talent on the horizon. This year's artists have been chosen by faculty members from MFA programs throughout California. Also on view: NIGHT MOVES: A series featuring window installations visible only after dark at the ICA: John Slepian: The Kiss and Asya Reznikov: Mapping.

The gallery is open until 10 pm.

www.sjica.org

San Jose Museum of Quilts and Textiles- 520 South First Street


On view: Weavings of War: Fabrics of Memory a traveling exhibition of textile works by mostly women from around the world depicting their reactions to war, Woven Witness: Afghan War Rugs an exhibition that explores the influence of war on traditional Afghan rug design, and Patriot Art, an exhibit that reflects on the uses of patriotic imagery and allusion in both contemporary fiber art and historical textiles.

First Friday evening will be an informal question and answer session about the Afghan Freedom Quilt: Silenced Voices of the Afghan Diaspora and Afghan War Rugs with: Melanie Gadener, founding director of the Foundation for Self-Reliance and Hanifa Tokhi, board member at the Foundation for Self-Reliance and long-time activist on behalf of Afghan refugees.

www.sjquiltmuseum.org

MACLA/Movimiento de Arte y Cultura Latino Americana- 510 South First Street

Opening Reception: MACLA is pleased to announce its second Chicana/o Biennial -- an exhibition and public forum conceived to take inventory of and invite reflection every two years on the continuously emergent energy, critical edge, and aesthetic interventions within contemporary Chicano art.

www.maclaarte.org

Works San Jose - 451 South First Street

Opening Reception: eXact-O! - The art of the precision cut with Jesse Houlding, Marina Shterenberg, Charlene Tan, and Annie Vought.

www.workssanjose.org
Anno Domini // the second coming of Art & Design- 366 South First Street


Opening Reception: A Summer Cut for Medusa's Daughters a new body of work and third Anno Domini exhibition by mythical painter Alfonso Kellenberger.
Featuring MERCH an experimental-pop cello-rock band from San Francisco that has made many waves in a short amount of time with their ultra-catchy rocking songs. In October, MERCH will be releasing their first album 'Crash Boom Bash'--songs dealing with hopping freight trains, throwing water balloons at yuppies, sorcerors, and more.

 www.galleryAD.com
