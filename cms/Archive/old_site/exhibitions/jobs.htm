Internship Opportunities
The San Jose ICA seeks self-motivated and enthusiastic interns to assist with our ongoing exhibitions and public programs. Under the supervision of the Assistant Curator and the Director of Marketing, this internship provides a well-rounded experience of working in a non-profit contemporary art space.  Projects may include: archiving and documenting exhibitions and public programs, creating artist files, marketing and public relations research as well as assisting with day-to-day administrative tasks.

The ideal skills for this position include attention to detail, enthusiasm and interest in contemporary art, and the ability to follow through with long-term projects. Technical skills such as Photoshop, InDesign, Microsoft Word, Excel and Dreamweaver are highly desirable, though not necessary. Minimum time requirement is 16 hours a week.  While this is an unpaid internship, college credit can be applied.

Email your resume and cover letter expressing your interest and qualifications to:
sue(at)sjica(dot)org

About the ICA
The San Jose Institute of Contemporary Art (ICA) seeks to engage Bay Area audiences through innovative visual art exhibitions and educational programs. The ICA reflects and interprets the cultural and technical diversity of the region and helps us see the lives we are living through the art of our time. The exhibitions and programs are designed to build community-to make a space where people of all backgrounds and ages may explore challenging contemporary art and ideas in an engaging and lively atmosphere.

The ICA presents forward-looking, thought-provoking, and exciting contemporary art by emerging and established Bay Area artists. The ICA's mission is to promote greater awareness, understanding, and appreciation of contemporary art and its capacity to stimulate our imaginations and illuminate our lives, relationships, and communities.
