Please join us for the opening reception of NextNew2006: Art and Technology
and the
"Not-So-Grand" Opening of the New ICA

Saturday, August 12
6:00 - 8:00 pm
(Gallery will remain open until midnight)

Come and help us celebrate our new permanent home at 560 South First Street and preview what's in the future for the ICA. On view through September 16 in one of three renovated gallery spaces planned for the ICA’s new location, NextNew2006: Art and Technology focuses on the work of 10 artists working in electronic media. The exhibition is being presented in conjunction with the international symposium of the Inter-Society for Electronic Art (ISEA) and the ZeroOne San Jose Global Festival of Art on the Edge.

At the artists' reception from 6-8 pm, you will be able to preview floor plans of the ICA renovation, listen to music by DJ Tomatsu and sample delicious hors d'oeuvres from Parsley, Sage, Rosemary and Thyme.

Following the reception, the ICA will present A Painting Performance, a multi media, interactive street event by new media artist Clive McCarthy from 8:30 - 10:00 pm on South First Street in front of the former ICA. Mr. McCarthy will create a dynamic architectural portrait and utilize new technologies to create a relevant and exciting experience. His work is a unique combination of cutting-edge technology and traditional painting and music, performed in front of a live audience. First Street will be closed to traffic from San Salvador to Reed Streets.

The new ICA gallery will stay open until midnight. MACLA, the Quilt Museum, and Anno Domini will aso be open late that night with special presentations to celebrate the closing of the ZeroOne Festival.

N.B. Parking will be challenging - we recommend the City lot under Hiway 280 at First Street and Virginia Street next to Delia's Furniture
