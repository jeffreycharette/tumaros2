February 1, 6 - late
The Space Between & Mapping Time: Doug Glovaski

Images left to right: Mel Prest, Saba, Courtesy of the Artist and Gregory Lind Gallery, SF; Doug Glovaski, Five, Ink on paper, Courtesy of the Artist and Dolby Chadwick Gallery, SF

8PM ’til LATE- SoFA ART WALK galleries are open late - free
The SoFA District is So. First Street between San Carlos and E. Reed streets

San Jose Institute of Contemporary Art -560 South First Street
Opening reception in the Main Gallery: The Space Between, a group exhibition that visually delights, disrupts and disorients. Featuring work by Freddy Chandra, Brent Hallard, Linn Meyers, Gay Outlaw, Mel Prest and Nancy White. Opening reception in the Focus Gallery and Cardinale Project Room: Mapping Time: Doug Glovaski, a solo presentation of Glovaski's large-scale, intensely colored abstract paintings and works on paper. NIGHT MOVES: A series featuring window installations visible only after dark at the ICA: Elaine Buckholtz: Yellow Descending.

Anno Domini // the second coming of Art & Design - 366 South First Street
Artist’s Reception: VICTIMS new work by Barron Storey. Barron Storey, the legendary, iconic illustrator and graphic artist, debuts a new series of work depicting the damaged and the wounded in sculptural form as well as works on canvas. This evening’s soundtrack provided by DJ Nate Nothing.

MACLA - 510 South First Street
Opening Reception: Beyond Child’s Play: New work by Pilar Agüero-Esparza & Juan Carlos Quintana. This two person exhibition features recent stunning paintings and elegant mixed media installations by two of the most compelling mid-career Bay Area artists working today. Their artwork complements one another through layered artistic practices, sensitivity towards materials and shared iconography stemming from the world of children. Also: A Places and Spaces Sound Session featuring artists Places and Spaces Resident Chatos 1013, DJ set by Baron Zen, a HipHopElectroPunk performance by The F*cking Mutants-Dave Dub and MEGABUSIVE.

San Jose Museum of Quilts & Textiles -520 South First Street
On view: Marian Clayden: A Dyer's Hand, a retrospective of the phenomenal career of Marian Clayden, master dyer, textile artist and fashion designer. This exhibition spans her textile art designed for the wall through to her later works designed for the body. Her eveningwear is worn by some of the most fascinating and accomplished women of our time, including Bridget Fonda, Patti LaBelle, Meryl Streep, Elizabeth Taylor, Sigourney Weaver, and Catherine Zeta-Jones. First Friday at 8pm come see a screening of the new documentary film by Yoshiko Wada Shibori: Celebrating 400 Years of Japanese Artisan Design. This film provides a fascinating look at two families who settled along the 'eastern sea road' in Japan in 1608 and how they developed their own innovative fabric dyeing techniques, ultimately creating magnificent designs.

Space47 - 47 E. William Street (between 1st & 2nd streets)
Opening reception: Horse Backs: Photographs by Mimi Plumb. Plumb's new works are a series of photographs responding to cynical times.

Works San Jose - 451 South First Street
Super SideScroller Game, Music, and Projection Night! Play the games! Hear the music! Sway to a mesmerizing beat as all manner of digital meanies are blasted from the sky! Guitar hero too, poetry in projections, rhythmic synchronized pixel art, and other surprises!

7pm - 9pm at KALEID gallery - 88 South Fourth Street (at San Fernando)
Artists’ Reception: Abstract painter Wendy Lowengrub and photographer Cindy Stokes provides complimentary views of form and pattern of nature through their respective mediums. Cindy Stokes exhibits photographs from her Dynamic Form series with an accompanying book. Wendy Lowengrub's Glaciology exhibit is a series of paintings inspired by a trip to the Souther Patagonian Ice Fields in Chile and Argentina. Featuring Isoscelles a classic jazz paino trio with definite leanings toward Bill Evans, Herbie Hancock et alia, playing new and vintage standards.

Connoisseur 533- 533 South Market Street

On view: Mixed Media by local artist Ivan. The work presented will be a combination of many mediums, ranging from sceneries, small scale fabrication, graffiti, portraits, illustrations and sculptures. Also featuring live painting and music.
