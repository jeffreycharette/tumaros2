2005 - 2006 Discussion Schedule

Talking Art: How to Play the Game
A series of salon-style discussions about the business of art - making it, collecting it, and appreciating it.

New Format:
FALL session, Sept 15, Oct 13, Nov 10
SPRING session, Mar 9, April 13, May 11
SUMMER session; 3 portfolio reviews
WHEN:
Thurdays: 7:00 pm discussion
WHERE:
The San Jose Institute of Contemporary Art
unless otherwise noted
HOW MUCH:
Free for ICA members and $5 for non-members

Thursday, September 15, 2005
Who's Who
Artists, dealers, curators, consultants, collectors, and critics are all part of the art “ecosystem.” These individuals will explain their roles in the system.
Panelists include: Robert Brady, artist; Heather Marx, Director, Heather Marx Gallery; Alison Bing, art writer; Wanda Kownacki, collector; Cathy Kimball, curator.
Location: SoFA Lounge, 372 S. First Street, San Jose

Thursday, October 13, 2005
Holding Patterns: The Art of Collecting
From the museum curator to the first-time buyer, from the all-inclusive to the finely focused, a cross section of collectors discuss where, why and how they collect art.
Panelists include: Mike Bewley, collector; Claire Carlevaro, Art Exchange Gallery; Joelle Connolly, Mary Zlot and Associates; JoAnne Northrup, Senior Curator, San Jose Museum of Art
Location: SoFA Lounge, 372 S. First Street

Thursday, November 10, 2005
Public Art Projects and Politics
What is news worthy and art worthy on the public art scene? How is public art funded, placed, maintained and received?
Panelists include: Barbara Goldstein, City of San Jose Office of Cultural Affairs and others TBA

Special Engagement:
Thursday, February 9, 2006
ARTreaching into China
A panel of renowned local artists and arts representatives will speak about an October, 2005 artistic exchange to Beijing, Xian and Shanghai and the experience and understanding of how art binds us together.

Participating panelists are Flo Oy Wong, installation mixed media artist; Lenore Chinn, a San Francisco-based artist; consultant and curator; Joyce Iwasaki, President of the Board of Directors of Arts Council Silicon Valley; Jane Place, a painter from Seattle, WA and an adjunct professor of art at Antioch University; and Nina Koepcke, a San Jose-based ceramic sculptor and painter.

Thursday, March 9, 2006
Sizzle: Out of the Frying Pan and into the Fire
This condensed version of his semester-long course will help you prepare for a career as a professional, working artist.
Presenter: Ray Beldner, Artist and Teacher

Thursday, April 13, 2006
The Gallery Game
Exhibition venues have traditionally been the principle means by which artists reach the public. Representatives from commercial galleries, non-profit spaces and alternative venues talk about their diverse programs.
Panelists: TBA

Thursday, May 11, 2006
To Go or Not to Go? MFA Programs Exposed
What is an MFA degree all about anyway? Artists who teach in MFA programs throughout the region talk about their unique curricula and the next generation of working artists.
Panelists: TBA

Portfolio Reviews (Three this year!)
Thursdays: June 8, July 6, August 24, 2006
A panel of curators, art dealers, teachers, and artists will provide 20-minute individual critiques to the first twenty artists to register. Each artist will have two critiques.

You are only eligible to participate in ONE of the summer portfolio reviews. These are special closed sessions. Only ICA members who have pre-registered may attend. Preference will be given to artists who have attended the spring session of Talking Art. Registration begins May 11, 2006. Cost: $25.

Reviewers include: Kathryn Dunlevie, artist; Anjee Helstrup, Associate Director, MACLA; Susan Hillhouse, curator, Museum of Art and History, Santa Cruz; Archana Horsting, Executive Director, KALA Institute; Karen Kienzle, curator, de Saisset Museum; Tony May, artist; David Middlebrook artist and teacher; Chuck Mobley, SF Camerawork; David Pace, photographer and teacher; Melissa Pickford, Director, Monterey Peninsula College Art Gallery; George Rivera, Director, Triton Museum: Michele Scott, independent curator; Stan Welsh, artist and teacher
