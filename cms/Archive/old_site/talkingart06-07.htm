2006 - 2007 Discussion Schedule

Talking Art: How to Play the Game
A series of salon-style discussions about the business of art - making it, collecting it, and appreciating it.

New Format:
FALL session, Oct 12, Nov 30, Dec 14, 2006
SPRING session, Mar 8, April 12, May 10, 2007
SUMMER session; 3 portfolio reviews
WHEN:
Thurdays: 7:00 pm discussion

The San Jose Institute of Contemporary Art
560 South First Street, San Jose

Portfolio Reviews (Three this year!)
Thursdays: June 14, July 12, August 9, 2007
A panel of curators, art dealers, teachers, and artists will provide 20-minute individual critiques to the first twenty artists to register. Each artist will have two critiques.

You are only eligible to participate in ONE of the summer portfolio reviews. These are special closed sessions. Only ICA members who have pre-registered may attend. Registration begins May 10, 2007. Cost: $25.

Reviewers include: Kathy Aoki, artist and Art Faculty member of Santa Clara University; Renee Billingslea, artist and Art Faculty member of Santa Clara University; Kelly Detweiler, artist and Art Faculty member of Santa Clara University; Ed Gilbert, Paule Anglim Gallery; Carrie Lederer, Curator of Exhibitions, Bedford Gallery; Heather Marx, Owner and Director, Heather Marx Gallery; Signe Mayfield, Curator, Palo Alto Art Center; Francis Mill, Director, Hackett-Freedman Gallery; JoAnne Northrup, Senior Curator, San Jose Museum of Art; Steve Pon, Exhibitions Coordinator, SFMOMA Artists Gallery; David Salow, Associate Director, Hackett-Freedman Gallery;

FOR MORE INFO: call 408-283-8155 or email info@sjica.org

Past Talking Art Discussions:

Thursday, October 12, 2006
Holding Patterns: The Art of Collecting
Whether you are just starting a collection or fine tuning the scope of a growing collection, this cross section of panelists discuss where, why and how they collect art as well as the importance of the Bay Area’s diverse collections.

Panelists include: David Henry, private collector and art consultant; ; Dan Keegan, Oshman Executive Director, San Jose Museum of Art; Jason Linetsky, Collection Manager, The Anderson Collection; and others TBA

NEW DATE: Thursday, November 30, 2006
A conversation with Darren Waterston

Painter, muralist and installation artist Darren Waterston will discuss his current project at the San Jose Institute of Contemporary Art with Executive Director Cathy Kimball.

Thursday, December 14, 2006
Residencies within Reach
Representatives and artists from some of the Bay Area’s hottest Artist Residencies discuss their programs.

Panelists include: Lauren Davies, Exhibitions and Public Programs Manager, Kala Art Institute, Berkeley; Mitra Fabian, Artist-in-Residency Coordinator, Works/San Jose, San Jose; Sarah Klein, Artist; Dennis O'Leary, Executive Director, Djerassi Resident Artists Program, Woodside; and Gary Sangster, Executive Director, Headlands Center for the Arts, Sausalito

Thursday, March 8, 2007
MFA Programs Exposed

What is an MFA degree all about anyway? Artists who teach in MFA programs and alumni from throughout the region talk about their unique curricula and the next generation of working artists.

Panelists: Rosana Castrillo Diaz, alumna, Mills MFA program; Amy Ellingson, Associate Professor of Painting, San Francisco Art Institute; Jeanne Finley, Professor of Media Studies, California College of Arts, San Francisco; and Lucy Puls, Professor of Art and Department Chair, UC Davis

Thursday, April 12, 2007 - held at Theatre on San Pedro Square, Downtown San Jose
The Gallery Game
Galleries have traditionally been the principle means by which artists reach the public. Representatives from diverse Bay Area commercial galleries talk about their individual directions, exhibitions and artists.

Panelists Include: Julie Casemore, Stephen Wirtz Gallery; Jack Fischer, Jack Fischer Gallery; Ellen Mahoney, John Berggruen Gallery; Don Soker, Don Soker Contemporary Art Gallery; and Michelle Townsend, Spur Projects, Portola Valley

Thursday, May 10, 2007
Art Primer: Presenting Yourself as a Professional Artist

Jamie Brunson and Cynthia Lait are exhibiting painters who teach professional practices workshops to artists. Their courses include writing statements, profiles and press releases; developing business practices and marketing plans; building a web presence and setting career goals.
